Client: Dean Gregory — Flowing Wood Designs

Project: Flowing Wood

Project: Flowing Wood

Documentary / Human Story

About

Flowing Wood is a short documentary on Dean Gregory, a travelling artist living on the remote northwest coastline of Western Australia. It follows how he gathers the sand, rock and feeling of the places he stays and works them into his paintings, and how art became a way back to the water after losing a close friend to a shark attack. A quiet study of one man, his craft and the country that shapes both.

Approach

Shot solo over two weeks on a remote stretch of coast. No crew, no budget, just a camera, the subject and the conditions. The film was built around the interview first, then carried by the landscape and Dean's process: static frames, water and drone work, and macro detail of the art coming to life. The wind, salt and isolation were never fought. They became part of the texture, and part of what makes it Flowing Wood.

Impact

Flowing Wood went on to screen at three film festivals: Noosa International Surf Film Festival, the Anglet International Surf Film Festival in France, and the Byron Bay International Film Festival, where it was awarded Best Byron Bay Film. A first documentary made before the maker felt ready, and proof of what a single, committed point of view can carry.
